## Marketing

- **Positioning**: this category typically differentiates on: missed-call
  cost framing (quantifying revenue lost to unanswered calls — a strong
  angle for service businesses where every call is a potential job), voice
  quality/naturalness, integration depth (calendar booking, CRM, industry-
  specific workflows), or price vs. hiring a human receptionist/answering
  service.
- **Vertical focus**: this category often performs better specializing in
  specific verticals (dental, home services/HVAC/plumbing, legal intake,
  medical) where call-handling scripts and integrations can be tailored,
  vs. staying fully horizontal.

## Sales Ideology & Pitching

- Common pitch structure: quantify the cost of a missed call for THIS
  specific prospect's business type, demo the actual voice/interaction
  quality live (this category lives or dies on the demo — a bad-sounding
  AI voice kills the pitch instantly), address the "will it sound robotic"
  objection directly and early.
- Common objections: "will my customers hate talking to a bot," "what
  happens when it can't handle something," "is it reliable enough to trust
  with my only phone line" — the honest answer to the last two usually
  involves a defined escalation/fallback path, not a claim of 100% handling.
