# Strategic Planning (department definition)

Owns the medium/long horizon — where the business is headed, not what
happens this week. Distinct from R&D (which is "how do we build it") and
Marketing (which is "how do we talk about it") — Strategic Planning is
"should we be doing this at all, and what does winning look like."

## Core responsibilities

- Setting direction: target market, growth priorities, what NOT to pursue
- Pivot decisions (Chaos Merchant's own format-pivot + autonomous-sourcing
  decision, documented in this repo's HANDOFF.md, is a real example of
  exactly this kind of call)
- Cross-department prioritization when resources are constrained
- Risk assessment at the business-model level (not day-to-day operational risk)

## Typical KPIs to track

- Progress against whatever the current strategic bets actually are (these
  are business-specific, defined in each business's own note)
- Time-to-decision on pivots/major calls (are decisions getting made, or
  stalling)

## Cadence

Monthly or quarterly — this is deliberately NOT a weekly cadence department;
if it's changing weekly, that's usually a sign something is actually an
R&D or Marketing decision, not a strategic one.
