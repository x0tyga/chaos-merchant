# Sales Ideology & Pitching (department definition)

Owns converting an interested prospect (handed off from Outreach, or
inbound via Marketing) into a paying customer — and the underlying
philosophy of HOW that business sells, not just the mechanics.

## Core responsibilities

- The pitch itself: what's the core value proposition, in what order, to
  whom
- Objection handling — the known objections for this business and the
  actual answers to them
- Pricing/packaging philosophy (not just the number — the reasoning: value-
  based, cost-plus, competitor-anchored, etc.)
- Close mechanics: what actually needs to happen for a prospect to become a
  customer (contract, onboarding call, self-serve signup, etc.)

## Typical KPIs to track

- Conversion rate (qualified conversation → customer)
- Average deal size / contract value
- Sales cycle length
- Win/loss reasons (why deals are actually won or lost — the qualitative
  half of this, not just the rate)

## Cadence

Review win/loss reasons monthly at minimum — this is where the pitch
philosophy actually gets tested against reality and should evolve.
