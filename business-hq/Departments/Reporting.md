# Reporting (department definition)

Owns turning raw operational/performance data into something a human can
actually act on. Distinct from Accounting (which owns money specifically) —
Reporting covers performance metrics: what's working, what isn't, and
whether the numbers actually support the story being told about the business.

## Core responsibilities

- Defining what gets measured for each business (the KPI set)
- Regular reporting cadence (what gets reported, how often, to whom — even
  if "whom" is just [[You]])
- Flagging when a metric moves outside its expected range, not just logging
  the number
- Keeping historical data comparable (same definitions over time, so
  month-over-month actually means something)

## Typical KPIs to track

- Whatever each business's own KPIs are (defined in that business's note) —
  Reporting's own KPI is really "is the reporting itself accurate, timely,
  and actually read/used"

## Cadence

Whatever cadence fits the business — weekly for fast-moving content/ops
businesses, monthly for slower-moving ones. Consistency matters more than
frequency.
