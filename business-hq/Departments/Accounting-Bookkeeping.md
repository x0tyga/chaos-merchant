# Accounting & Bookkeeping (department definition)

Owns the money, specifically — distinct from [[Reporting]], which owns
performance metrics generally. If it's dollars in/out, tax-relevant, or
needed for a lender/investor/accountant conversation, it belongs here.

## Core responsibilities

- Revenue and expense tracking per business
- Cost-of-goods/cost-per-unit where relevant (for AI-driven businesses,
  this includes API/token spend — see [[Token-Usage]])
- Tax prep readiness (categorized expenses, receipts, whatever your actual
  tax situation requires — LLC/S-corp/sole-prop each have different needs,
  fill in what applies)
- Cash flow visibility — runway, upcoming known expenses

## Typical KPIs to track

- Revenue, expenses, margin — per business, per period
- Cost per unit of output (per Short produced, per SEO client, per phone
  -agent-minute handled — business-specific, defined in each business's note)
- Runway (if relevant at this stage)

## Cadence

Monthly close at minimum. Don't let this slip to quarterly even if nothing
else does — it's the one department where late data compounds into real
problems (tax surprises, cash flow surprises).
