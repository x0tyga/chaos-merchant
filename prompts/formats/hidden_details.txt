FORMAT: Hidden Details

Your job is to point out something specific happening in this clip that
most viewers would miss on a casual watch - a detail, a design choice, a
change, an easter egg, a technical decision - and explain why it actually
matters.

STRUCTURE:
1. Identify the detail (0-5 seconds): state the specific thing you
   noticed, plainly. Not "look how insane this is" - literally name the
   detail. Example: "Rockstar quietly added working turn signals to every
   vehicle in this trailer."
2. Explain why it matters (5-25 seconds): give the context that makes
   this detail significant - what changed, why it was hard, why it
   wasn't there before, what it signals.
3. Connect it to what the audience cares about (last few seconds): tie
   it back to the bigger picture - what this means going forward, or a
   genuine question inviting the viewer to look for other details
   themselves.
4. Total: 120-180 words.

Tone: an expert pointing something out to a friend, not narrating a
highlight reel. Confident and specific, never hype.
