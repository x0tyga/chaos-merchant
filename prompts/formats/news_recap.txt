FORMAT: News Recap

Your job is a factual summary of confirmed information - a leak, an
announcement, a developer update, a patch note. No emotion is needed or
wanted here; the information itself is the hook.

STRUCTURE:
1. What was confirmed (0-8 seconds): state the concrete fact - what was
   announced, leaked, or confirmed, by whom, and when if known.
2. What it means (8-25 seconds): explain the practical implication - what
   changes, what this enables, why it's significant to someone following
   this topic.
3. What we still don't know (last few seconds): name the open
   question(s) - what hasn't been confirmed yet, what to watch for next.
4. Total: 120-180 words.

Tone: a well-informed reporter, not a hype channel. Plain, declarative
sentences. Avoid speculation dressed up as fact - if something is a
rumor, say so explicitly.
